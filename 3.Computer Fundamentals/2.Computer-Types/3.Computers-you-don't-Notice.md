# Computers You Don’t Notice (Hidden & Everyday Devices)

## Overview

Sophia realized that many computers are **not visible like laptops or desktops** — they are hidden inside everyday objects and devices.

---

## Main Types of Everyday Computers

| Type | What it is | Examples |
|------|------------|----------|
| Smartphone | Pocket-sized computer focused on battery and connectivity | iPhone, Android phones |
| Tablet | Touch-based computer with a larger screen | iPad, drawing tablets |
| IoT Device | Internet-connected device with a specific function | Smart thermostat, doorbell, fitness tracker |
| Embedded Computer | Built into another device to control one task | Coffee machine controller, door sensor, lamp dimmer |

---

## Key Concept: IoT vs Embedded Systems

### IoT (Internet of Things)
- Connected to the internet
- Sends and receives data
- Can be controlled remotely

👉 Example: Smart thermostat you control from your phone

---

### Embedded Systems
- Built inside a device
- Often NOT connected to the internet
- Runs a single dedicated function
- Works silently for years

👉 Example: Sensor inside an automatic door

---

## Real-World Example

Sophia walked through automatic doors every day without realizing:

- A hidden embedded computer detects movement  
- It sends a signal to the door motor  
- The door opens automatically  

---

## Key Insight

Modern computing is not always visible — it is deeply embedded in everyday life, working silently in the background.

---

## Key Takeaway

Computers exist everywhere: from smartphones in your pocket to invisible embedded systems inside machines you use daily.
