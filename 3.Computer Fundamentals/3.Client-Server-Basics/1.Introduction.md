## Introduction to Client-Server Basics

In the early days, computers worked as **standalone systems**. Each computer stored its own data, ran its own programs, and did not communicate with other machines.

As technology evolved, organisations began connecting computers together to **share information and resources over distance**. This marked the beginning of early networks such as:

- ARPANET  
- CYCLADES  
- NPL Network  
- NSFNET  

These networks eventually formed the foundation of the modern Internet.

### Why This Matters
Once computers were connected, they started to specialise:
- Some computers **request services**
- Others **provide services**

This created the **Client–Server Model**, which is the backbone of how the internet works today.
