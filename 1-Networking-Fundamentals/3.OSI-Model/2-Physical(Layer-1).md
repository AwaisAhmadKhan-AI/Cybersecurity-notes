# OSI Layer 1 - Physical Layer

## Overview

The **Physical Layer** is the first and lowest layer of the OSI Model.

It is responsible for the physical connection between devices and the transmission of raw data across a network.

---

## Main Purpose

The Physical Layer handles the actual movement of data using physical hardware and transmission media.

Data is transmitted as:

- Electrical signals
- Light signals
- Radio signals

These signals represent binary data:

- `1`
- `0`

---

## Responsibilities of the Physical Layer

### Data Transmission
- Transfers raw bits between devices.

### Physical Connections
- Establishes the physical link between network devices.

### Signal Conversion
- Converts digital data into signals that can travel through a transmission medium.

---

## Components of the Physical Layer

Examples of physical networking hardware include:

- Ethernet cables
- Fiber optic cables
- Network connectors
- Hubs
- Repeaters
- Wireless radio transmitters

---

## Example

When a computer sends data through an Ethernet cable:

1. Data is converted into electrical signals.
2. Signals travel through the cable.
3. The receiving device interprets those signals as binary data (`1s` and `0s`).

---

## Key Characteristics

- Lowest layer of the OSI Model.
- Deals with hardware and physical connections.
- Transmits raw binary data.
- Does not understand the meaning of the data being transmitted.

---

## Key Takeaway

The **Physical Layer (Layer 1)** is responsible for transmitting raw bits across physical media such as Ethernet cables, fiber optics, and wireless signals. It forms the foundation of all network communication.
