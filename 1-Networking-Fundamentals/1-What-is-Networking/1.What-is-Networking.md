
					**Introduction to Networks**

**	What is a Network?**

A network is a group of connected devices that communicate and share information with each other.

	****Simple Example****

Friendship circle = people connected by common interests.
Computer network = devices connected to exchange data.

            **Examples of Networks in Daily Life**
1.Public transportation systems
2.National electricity grids
3.Communication with neighbours
4.Postal and delivery systems
5.Networks in Computing

	In computing, a network consists of connected technological devices such as:

1.Computers and laptops
2.Smartphones
3.Security cameras
4.Traffic lights
5.Smart farming equipment
6.Servers and data centres

	A network can contain:

1.As few as 2 devices
2.As many as billions of devices

	**Why Do We Use Networks?**

	Networks allow devices to:

1.Communicate with each other
2.Share information
3.Access resources and services
4.Exchange data efficiently

	Example:

Your smartphone uses networks to access websites, applications, and online services.

	**Importance of Networks in Everyday Life**

	Networks are used for:

1.Weather data collection
2.Electricity distribution
3.Traffic management
4.Internet communication
5.Smart city infrastructure
	
	**Importance of Networking in Cybersecurity**

	Networking is a fundamental concept in cybersecurity because:

1.Most cyber attacks occur over networks.
2.Security professionals must understand how devices communicate.
3.Protecting networks helps prevent unauthorized access and data theft.

**	Key Takeaway **

A network is a collection of connected devices that communicate and share information. Networks power modern technology and form the foundation of cybersecurity.
