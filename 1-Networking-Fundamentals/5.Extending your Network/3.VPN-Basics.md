# VPN Basics

## What is a VPN?

A **Virtual Private Network (VPN)** is a technology that creates a **secure connection (tunnel)** between devices over the internet.

👉 It allows devices on different networks to communicate as if they are on the same private network.

---

## How VPN Works (Simple Idea)

Normally:
- Devices on different networks cannot directly access each other

With VPN:
- A secure “tunnel” is created over the internet
- Devices inside this tunnel form a private network

---

## Example

- Office #1 (Network #1)
- Office #2 (Network #2)
- VPN Network (Secure tunnel connection)

Even though they are physically separate:
- VPN connects them as if they are one private network

---

## Key Features of VPN

### 1. Secure Tunnel
- Data travels through an encrypted path
- Prevents outsiders from reading traffic

---

### 2. Private Network Formation
- Devices connected via VPN form a virtual private network
- Only VPN members can communicate inside it

---

## Benefits of VPN

### 1. Connects Remote Networks
- Offices in different locations can share resources
- Example: servers, files, systems

---

### 2. Privacy
- VPN encrypts data
- Prevents others from reading your traffic
- Useful on public Wi-Fi

---

### 3. Anonymity
- Hides your real network activity from ISP
- Helps protect identity in restricted environments

⚠️ Note:
- VPN provider can still log your activity

---

## VPN Technologies

### 1. PPP (Point-to-Point Protocol)
- Provides authentication and encryption
- Uses private keys and certificates
- Cannot route data alone

---

### 2. PPTP (Point-to-Point Tunneling Protocol)
- Allows PPP data to travel over networks
- Easy to set up
- Weak encryption (less secure)

---

### 3. IPSec (Internet Protocol Security)
- Encrypts data using IP system
- Strong security
- More complex to configure

---

## Real-World Use

VPNs are used to:

- Connect company offices securely
- Protect users on public Wi-Fi
- Provide secure access to internal systems
- Allow secure learning platforms (like TryHackMe)

---

## Key Takeaway

A VPN creates a **secure encrypted tunnel over the internet**, allowing devices in different locations to communicate safely as if they are on the same private network.
